## üö® Red Flags to Watch For

**Stop and address these issues before proceeding**:

- ‚ö†Ô∏è Client can't articulate clear goals or success metrics
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Budget is unrealistic for scope
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Timeline is impossible
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Client wants to "figure out content later"
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Scope keeps expanding without discussion
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Multiple decision makers with different visions
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Client unresponsive during discovery phase
- ‚ö†Ô∏è "We want it exactly like [competitor] but different"
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Client expects features beyond your skill level
- ‚ö†Ô∏è No clear point of contact or approval process

**If you encounter red flags**: Have an honest conversation with the client to resolve issues before starting development.

## üí° Tips for Successful Requirements Gathering

**DO**:
- ‚úÖ Use this checklist as a living document
- ‚úÖ Update it as you receive items
- ‚úÖ Share progress with client regularly
- ‚úÖ Set clear deadlines for content delivery
- ‚úÖ Provide examples of what you need
- ‚úÖ Offer to help source missing items (at additional cost)
- ‚úÖ Document everything in writing

**DON'T**:
- ‚ùå Start development with incomplete requirements
- ‚ùå Assume client will provide missing items later
- ‚ùå Move forward with "placeholder" content plan
- ‚ùå Skip items because client says they're "not important"
- ‚ùå Accept vague answers to critical questions
- ‚ùå Ignore red flags or warning signs
